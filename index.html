Housing In Melbourne: Beyond The Price Tag
[Python]

This machine learning project predicts house prices in Melbourne using factors such as suburb, building area, latitude, and longitude. To achieve this, data pipelines were created for cleaning, feature encoding, and outlier management, successfully reducing the Mean Absolute Error (MAE) from $276K to $159K. After developing and evaluating four different models, Ridge Regression was selected as the optimized final model. The solution was then deployed as a Flask API on Render and as an interactive Jupyter Widget on Google Colab, allowing for real-time predictions.

Analysing Student's Mental Health In Japan
[PostgreSQL]

This project explores the mental health challenges faced by international students studying in Japan, drawing on a 2018 university study. Analysis of the dataset reveals that international students are more susceptible to depression and stress than their domestic peers, with key factors including social connectedness and length of stay influencing their mental well-being. The findings underline the importance of addressing cultural adaptation and support systems to enhance the overall mental health of these students.

Time-Series Volatility Forecasting
[Python]

The project involved time-series volatility forecasting using Python and the AlphaVantage API, focusing on Microsoft (MSFT) and Apple (AAPL). Key activities included conducting exploratory data analysis to compare stock trends and risks, developing a GARCH-based model for forecasting Microsoft’s volatility, and deploying an interactive Streamlit web app on Render. This app allows users to access real-time stock volatility predictions, compute returns, and visualize trend forecasts.
